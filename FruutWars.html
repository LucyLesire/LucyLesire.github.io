Fruut Wars

For the course Game Projects, we were assigned a group of 2 programmers and 3 artists with the goal of making a vertical slice of a couch game.
We chose to make a Couch PVP shooter, in Unreal Engine 4.27 where you play as fruits and vegetables.

The goal of the game is to kill eachother by firing fruits & vegetables, pick up better weapons and avoid falling in the water.

Learning

During this course I have learned how to use C++ with Unreal Engine.
Furthermore I have also learned how to work on a big project with a (relativly) big team.

Finally I have learned how to create and setup a local multiplayer game in unreal.

Unreal Engine 4

We chose Unreal Engine 4 because of it's built in multiplayer features, particle system and blueprint functionality. We wrote most of our functionallity in C++ and some minor alterations in blueprints.

My role in the project was very broad, due to the small team size, I had to take half of the work on me. My work contained the following:

  • Writing and implementing the health component.
  • Writing and implementing the energy, dash and shield logic.
  • Writing and implementing the Powerups.
  • Writing and implementing the "Tile shaking" and "Tile sinking logic".
  • Creating and implementing the Animation Blueprint logic.
  • Creating and implementing "Dr Jalape√±o", "Lady Melon" & "Commisioner Potato"
  • Writing and implementing "The Corn Minigun", "The Lemon Bomb" & "The Coconut Ball".
  • Implementing the HUD".

Weekly dev

First week: Moving & Shooting

Second week: Powerups

Third week: Tiles

Fourth week: Death

Fifth week: Animations & HUD

Sixth week: Weapons!

Seventh week: Polish

My experiences

Week 1: When starting our project, we chose to first get out some base classes that functioned as blueprint for the rest of the game to come. I started work on the healthcomponent, as my teammate took the movement and shooting on his plate. Furthermore I made sure that we had a working gamemode.

Week 2: Next came the programming of the powerups, as besides shooting they were a fundamental part of the game! In order for me to create the powerups I needed to write the logic for energy, dashing and shields.

Week 3: At this point in our game, we had gotten some of the art assets already, so we could change from a boring little box level to an actual pretty and funtional level. But this also ment that I needed to start work on the environmental effects of tile shaking and tile falling.

Week 4: with tile sinking in place, it was time to make our player killable, it wouldn't be much of shooting game without it. One of the ways for the characters to die is by falling of the map, and as the map gets increasingly smaller, it also gets increasingly easier to fall off.

Week 5: At the start of week 5, we were already quite deep into the project, and art came rolling in with it, including a new character and animations! It was my job to hook these up and make sure that the blended nicely! On top of that we also added a HUD, so that the player can see their bullets, health, shield and energy!

Week 6: In this week, the production fase was coming to an end, so it was time for us to polish the gameloop and add much functionality for the game to be as fun as possible! I took care of adding 4 new weapons, including the Corn Shotgun, Grape Minigun, The Coconut and The Lemon Bomb.

Week 7 & 8:With the production fase out of the way it was time for the final step, polishing, in the next to weeks I mainly took care of a lot of bugs that popped up during production, but I also refactored the HUD to be more visible and less overwhelming in information, and I added tons of sounds and particles to existing weapons and powerups!
