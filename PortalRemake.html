Portal Remake

For the course, Graphics Programming 2, we have to (re)make a game in the Overlord Engine, a custom engine created by our teacher, Thomas Goussaert.
I chose to remake portal as it fell within the category of games we were allowed to make and I have loved portal since I was a kid.

The goal of this project is to remake a level, add in the basic mechanics and apply: Animations, Ambient Occlusion, Shadow Mapping, Shaders, Picking, Post-Processing and more.

Portal version 3 (with recursion)

Prototyping

Before starting the actual project, I decided to prototype the portal mechanic in Unity first.

I started with implementing the portal shader itself, I did this by adding 2 cameras int the scene that rotate and position themselves as the player is to their linked portal. I then set this view to texture of a plane on the portal, creating the illusion of a portal.
However this still seemed a little off, it was showing the view of the camera attached through the portal (making the portal visible), rather then actually showing the view you would see through the portal.
To fix this, I mapped the view of the camera that you see through the portal to the plane of the portal, creating the correct effect.
After this I implemented the Teleporting itself. This I achieved by registering the traveller, teleporting them to the location of the linked portal, unregistering them from the first portal and registering them to the second portal.
Finally I implemented recursion, when you looked through a portal, you could see an infinite amount of portals, which looked a bit weird. I fixed this by implemnting recursion, with a set amount. The recursion will map the next image smaller and smaller per iteration of portal, until the final one, which is drawn black.
